Manchurian

A popular Indo-Chinese dish made of crispy vegetable or chicken balls tossed in a tangy, spicy sauce.

Ready In:

45 minutes

Serves:

2-3

Runtime:

45 minutes

Ingredients


For the Manchurian Balls:

1 cup grated cabbage

1/2 cup grated carrot

1/4 cup finely chopped capsicum

2 tbsp all-purpose flour

2 tbsp cornflour

1 tsp ginger-garlic paste

1/2 tsp black pepper

Salt to taste

Oil for frying

For the Sauce:

1 tbsp oil

1 tsp garlic (minced)

1 green chili (finely chopped)

1/4 cup onion (finely chopped)

1/4 cup capsicum (finely chopped)

1 tbsp soy sauce

1 tbsp chili sauce

1 tbsp tomato ketchup

1/2 tsp vinegar

1/2 tsp black pepper

1/2 tsp sugar

1/2 cup water

1 tsp cornflour mixed with 2 tbsp water

Spring onions for garnish

Directions:

Step 1:


In a bowl, mix grated cabbage, carrot, capsicum, all-purpose flour, cornflour, ginger-garlic paste, black pepper, and salt. Form small balls.

Step 2:


Heat oil in a pan and deep-fry the balls until golden brown. Drain and set aside.

Step 3:


In another pan, heat 1 tbsp oil. Add minced garlic and green chili. Sauté for a few seconds.

Step 4:


Add chopped onion and capsicum. Stir-fry for 2 minutes.

Step 5:


Add soy sauce, chili sauce, tomato ketchup, vinegar, black pepper, sugar, and salt. Mix well.

Step 6:


Pour in 1/2 cup water and bring to a simmer. Add the cornflour slurry and stir until the sauce thickens.

Step 7:


Add the fried Manchurian balls and toss until well coated in the sauce.

Step 8:


Garnish with chopped spring onions and serve hot.
